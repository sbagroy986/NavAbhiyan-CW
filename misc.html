Survey Conducted at Jal Vihar - The Madrasi camp at Jal Vihar,Lajpat Nagar, New Delhi consists of thousands of Tamil domestic and class-four employees migrated from their home towns down south since many generations ago. Adult family members leave early for work, usually taking the older children for work and leaving the younger ones to take care of the infants at home. The adolescent children especially girls get neither education nor nutrition and no options to improve their lot either. In 2009 we conducted a baseline survey, under the sponsorship of Unniti Foundation, to collect the data to assess the basic requirements of this 'target' group.
The Findings are :

  • 98 % women work as domestic help. They are main bread winners of the family. Men folk are generally alcoholics, who beat their wives and children to get money for drinking

  • 70% of adolescent girls, even younger, are working as domestic help.

  • Few girls under the age of 10yrs. are going to nearby Schools of MCD or Sarv Shiksha Abhiyan, they carry bag load of books on their backs but their understanding of books, even simple reading is not upto the required levels

  • Literacy rate amongst girls is 20% only

  • Residents are not aware of Govt. schemes like Ladli etc. for the promotion of girl child. They look at girls as money generating machines for parents before marriage and for husband after marriage

  • 57% children are not going to any School and 20% have dropped School after failing to clear class 8th

  • Dropped out boys are just not doing any work. They shy away from going with their fathers in the morning for washing cars as they consider themselves as educated but they can’t get a job because they know nothing. They indulge in all sorts of thefts and other crimes in the neighbourhood

  • Few girls enrolled their names for vocational training if its classes are held between 1.30 to 3.30 only
