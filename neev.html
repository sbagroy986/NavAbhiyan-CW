Neev



Neev is our educational project.

  • Informal education for the illiterate and ignorant domestic workers living in JJ cluster around the Lajpat Nagar Railway station

  • To help their school-going children with their studies, home-work and exam preparation

  • Conceptualised with the sentiment of Nishkaam Karma by the like-minded members of the Bharatiya Yog Sansthan in Jangpura

  • Initial infra-structure from one of the members, followed by Shiv Mandir on Msjid Road and then the Basement of E-26 Jangpura Extension

  • Initial financial grant from Unniti Foundation India and continued support from the generous, well meaning and blessed section of society believing in the future of India

  • Started with 2 students and grew from strength to strength

  • Demand for pre-school classes for the younger children

  • Still number of needy far greater than those availing the services due to casual attitude of the parents- led to the branch centre at a hired jhuggi in the Madrasi camp





EWS Quota in Public Schools

  • At Neev we make the unaware and uneducated inhabitants of the jhuggi clusters aware of the government schemes and help them use them for the growth and development of their children

  • Every year we help them fill up their forms for Schools like Bahrtiya Vidya Bhavan, DPS, Banyan Tree, DTEA, Baba Nebraj School etc.



Awareness and Accessibility to the government schemes

  • Bills passed in favour of poor people never really reach them due to lack of awareness. We at Neev work as a bridge to make them aware

  • We help them get their birth certificates and income certificates to take advantage of the facilities provided





Sponsorship for the Needy

  • We have been able to secure sponsors for most of these children to ensure that these children's bright future prospects do not suffer for want of money

  • In some cases, we have been able to offer further support in financing the uniforms, books, bus fee etc.



Extra Curricular Activities

  • All significant festivals are celebrated to impart a sense of secularism and a national pride to this most deprived section

  • Recreational trips liven up the process of education and rounds-off the knowledge base required by covering various places like the India Gate, Rail Museum, Doll Museum, Children Park, Science museum, Sanskriti Kendra,Humauyun Tomb etc.

  • Birthday of each child is celebrated in the school and comemorated by a special birthday gift to each one. This is an effort to underline the specialness and importance of each child. It is to make them know how special and loved they are

  • Special activity class is organised every saturday to tap the tremndous art and crafts potential noticed in this group

  • The students staged the play' Kachra Rakshas' under the highly involved and committed direction of a team of SPA students. We are indeed grateful to them for all their efforts





Summer Camp

  • During their vacations we keep this restless and hyperactive lot busy by channelising their energies in creative activties. Either we conduct it ourselves at our own premises or we send them to the National Bal Bhavan

  • it is also a unique platform to help them with their Holiday assignment and ensure they understand and do their projects

  • At the same time volunteers from top-notch Public schools of Delhi contribute to this summer camp with their time and efforts. This serves the twin purpose of knowledge sharing amongst peer thereby bridging social gaps of economic strata and laying the foundation of another generation of Nav-Abhiyan members and Nishkaam karmayogis

  • All participants of this summer camp are awarded certificates of participation

  • Every year we help them fill up their forms for Schools like Bahrtiya Vidya Bhavan, DPS, Banyan Tree, DTEA, Baba Nebraj School etc.

  • Children of all sections of society work together, dance and sing. It is one long party where each precious little one is contributing and gaining at the same time

  • We concluded from these workshops that the youth revels in youth itself and infact their energies and dynamics have educated us beyond any school









Teacher's Welfare Programme

  • Most teachers are PG students. We encourage them enhance their qualifications

  • In July 2010 we enrolled 6 of them for basic computer training from NIIT

  • This has helped some of them secure better higher paying jobs & we always wish them all the success in life

  • In 2010 we organised a workshop for the personality developemnt by inviting professional from various fields

  • We selected and sent a panel of 2 teachers to NIMH Special school to educate ourselves in the way to educate and ways to deal with Students of Special needs

  • We also provide financial assistance to teachers to enhance their career. One teacher was sent to SHIKSHALAYA for personality development. One teacher sent for English speaking course



Health and Hygiene

  • We believe that only healthy bodies can have a healthy mind. Medical check-ups form an integral part of our school curriculum

  • We have a committed team of voluntary medical practitioners including reputed doctors

  • Dr. Shelly Chadha, ENT surgeon and Professor of ENT at the MAMC, Delhi is also a member of the Nav-abhiyan. She organised a special camp with a team of doctors from the Maulan Azad Medical college, New Delhi for a special check-up and free advice for the residents of the Madrasi camp at Lajpat Nagar

  • Similar camps have been organised by Dr.Deepa Kapur for Eye check-ups etc.

  • Another feature for all our students is the check-up and immunization programme by MAX India and regular follow-ups by them





Parents Awareness

  • On a regular basis we organise events to include the Parents in the school activities

  • We regularly hold sessions to update them on new Governmental & Non-governmental schemes that they can use to their and their families' benefits

  • A PTM ( Parent- Teacher Meeting) is held after almost every Unit test to keep them in tune with their ward's performance and their specific needs

  • We believe that we must partner with them in the interest of OUR children and their welfare

  • We use these PTMs and all other interaction sessions to also extend some education and awareness to them. For instance we invited the Bank Manager of the PNB for 1 such session and his interaction inspired many of the Parents to open bank accounts, cleared a lot of their reservations and myths about banks at large

  • Alcoholism amongst the male members was identified by us as the major issue that this strata faces. We at Neev engaged with 'The Delhi Inter-Group Alcoholics Anonymous' to assign a special Tamil speaking social worker who interacted with them at the Madrasi camp. There are some who are following-up with him on a regular basis
